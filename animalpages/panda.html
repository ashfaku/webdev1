Panda

Pandas are large, black-and-white bears native to China, known for their distinctive markings and gentle nature. They primarily live in bamboo forests in mountainous regions, where they spend most of their time eating bamboo, which makes up about 99% of their diet. Despite their classification as carnivores, they have evolved to rely almost entirely on plants. Pandas are solitary animals, communicating through vocalizations and scent markings. Due to habitat loss and low birth rates, they were once considered endangered, but conservation efforts have helped increase their population, and they are now classified as "vulnerable." Their playful behavior and unique appearance have made them a global symbol of wildlife conservation.
